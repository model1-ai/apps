ERP Resource Catalog Layer 0

Layer 0 -- Independently Deployable. The Resource Catalog has no cross-domain dependencies and can be loaded, validated, and populated before any other ERP block exists. Resources are cataloged things regardless of who owns them, where they are stored, or how they are accounted for.

1. Overview

The Resource Catalog generalizes the traditional "Item Catalog" to encompass everything an enterprise owns, uses, consumes, produces, assigns, or accounts for. This includes physical goods, services, financial instruments, information assets, and capacity.

The block answers three fundamental questions:

QuestionExamples
What does the enterprise deal with?Physical items for sale/purchase, fixed assets that depreciate, raw materials entering production
What non-physical things does it consume or provide?Services, licenses, information assets, financial instruments
What capacity constraints does it operate under?Labor hours, machine hours, storage space

The design uses a 3-level generalization hierarchy: an abstract root (erp-resource), three intermediate abstract branches (erp-tangible-resource, erp-intangible-resource, erp-capacity-resource), and nine concrete leaf universals. This structure enables uniform cataloging, costing, status tracking, and analytical aggregation across all resource types through the shared ancestor.

11. Analytical Value

The 3-level generalization hierarchy unlocks cross-domain aggregation that would be impossible with separate, unrelated catalogs.

Resource Consumption by Department

At Layer 1+, when resources are linked to org units, an artifact can aggregate physical items consumed, services procured, and labor hours allocated through the shared erp-resource root. A department that consumed 500 units of raw materials, 40 hours of consulting, and 160 hours of machine capacity reports all three as "resources consumed" in a single view -- with drill-down into each subtype.

Uniform Capacity Utilization Dashboard

The erp-capacity-utilization-calc computation applies identically across all capacity subtypes. Whether tracking person-hours, machine-hours, or warehouse pallet positions, the formula is the same. This enables a dashboard showing all constrained resources side by side, highlighting bottlenecks and driving rebalancing decisions.

Category-Based Cost Analysis

Because erp-resource-category-rel applies at the erp-resource level, categories can span tangible and intangible resources. A category like "IT Infrastructure" might contain fixed assets (servers), licenses (software), services (cloud hosting), and storage capacity (data center space). Aggregating at the category level gives a true total-cost view no single traditional ERP module provides.

12. Decision Points

3-Level Hierarchy for Analytical Power

A flat list of resource types (physical item, service, license, ...) loses the ability to aggregate at intermediate levels. The 3-level hierarchy enables queries like "total tangible resource cost" or "all intangible resources expiring this quarter" without enumerating every leaf type. The intermediate abstractions (erp-tangible-resource, erp-intangible-resource, erp-capacity-resource) carry properties common to their branch, avoiding property duplication on leaf types.

Tangible vs Intangible vs Capacity

The three-branch structure reflects a genuine ontological distinction:

  • Tangible resources have physical properties (weight, volume) and can be tracked by serial/lot. They exist in space.
  • Intangible resources have no physical form but may expire and require renewal. They exist as rights or representations.
  • Capacity resources represent available potential rather than a discrete thing. They are consumed by allocation over time, not by ownership transfer.

This branching determines which properties are inherited and which computations apply. Weight and volume are meaningless for a license; utilization rate is meaningless for a serialized physical item.

Resource Is NOT a Party

Resources are what parties transact with -- they are the objects of economic events, not the agents. A vendor sells resources; a department consumes them; a customer buys them. Conflating resources with parties would collapse the fundamental agent/object distinction in economic modeling. The Party Catalog is a separate Layer 0 block.

Standard Measurement Frames for Diverse Types

Rather than creating custom measurement frames, the catalog uses standard frames from the foundation bootstrap: std-weight, std-volume, std-currency, std-float, std-ratio, std-integer, std-quantity, std-time-duration, std-timestamp. This ensures interoperability with other ERP blocks and analytics without frame conversion logic.

Capacity Resources as a Distinct Category

Traditional ERP systems track items and sometimes services, but capacity (labor-hours, machine-hours, storage space) is usually managed in separate modules with separate data models. Elevating capacity to a first-class branch under erp-resource means capacity can be cataloged, classified, and analytically aggregated alongside items and services. The erp-capacity-utilization-calc computation applies uniformly to all three capacity subtypes through inheritance.

Category Hierarchy Separate from Generalization

The generalization hierarchy (erp-resource -> erp-tangible-resource -> erp-physical-item) defines what a resource IS ontologically. The category hierarchy (erp-resource-category via erp-category-parent-rel) defines how a resource is CLASSIFIED operationally. A physical item that IS a tangible resource might be CLASSIFIED under "Electronics > Components > Resistors". Both hierarchies coexist without conflict.

Financial Instruments -- Scope Boundary

erp-financial-instrument covers tradable or held-to-maturity instruments (bonds, securities, derivatives). Receivables and payables are explicitly excluded -- they are transactional positions created by economic events at Layer 1+, not catalog entries. Including them would conflate master data with transactional state.

The Resource Catalog can be loaded, validated, and fully populated as a standalone metadata app. No other ERP block needs to exist. Higher layers connect to it by creating relators that reference the resource universals defined here. This design ensures that the foundation of all resource-related operations -- cataloging, status management, categorization, and capacity tracking -- is stable before transactional complexity is layered on top.
