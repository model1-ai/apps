ERP Foundations — Ontological Architecture Overview

The ERP Foundations suite is a collection of 9 metadata apps (1 shared base + 8 domain apps) built on the Model1 ontology-driven platform. They establish the structural, financial, temporal, geographic, party, and resource primitives required by any enterprise resource planning system — plus two higher-layer domains (Budgeting and OKR/Goals) that compose them. A shared base app (app_erp_base) centralizes organization, tenant, users, and the cross-domain erp-goal foundation universal with its 11 properties. Domain apps contain no users, no org/tenant definitions — only their own app definition, universals, properties, and app-scoped foundation_role_assignments.

1. Suite Overview

The ERP Foundations suite follows a layered dependency architecture where each layer depends only on layers below it. This enables parallel development of foundation blocks and incremental composition into higher-level domains.

Layer Structure

Layer Purpose Apps Dependencies
Layer 0 Foundation Building Blocks — independently deployable primitives Org Structure, Chart of Accounts, Fiscal Calendar, Party Master, Resource Catalog, Location Master None (only Model1 platform infrastructure)
Layer 1 First Compositions — domains that compose Layer 0 blocks Budgeting Org Structure + Chart of Accounts + Fiscal Calendar
Layer 2 Transactional Domains — full process workflows OKR / Goals Org Structure + Party Master + Fiscal Calendar (Budget optional)
Key Principle: All six Layer 0 blocks can be deployed and validated independently. They share no hard structural dependencies (no cross-block generalizations or required compositions). Optional cross-block references (e.g., Party → Address from Location, GL Account → Cost Center from Org Structure) enhance functionality when multiple blocks are co-deployed but are never required.

7. Decision Points Log

Key ontological design decisions made across the suite. These decisions affect generalization hierarchies, composition choices, and analytical capabilities.

D1: Party-Role Separation

Decision: Employee, Vendor, and Customer are roles assigned via role-assignment-relator, not generalization specializations of Party.

Rationale: A single natural person can simultaneously hold multiple roles (an employee who is also a customer). Generalization would require separate entity instances for each role, breaking identity. Roles are lightweight assignments that can be added/removed without affecting the underlying party identity.

Affected apps: Party Master (Layer 0), all Layer 1+ consumer apps.

D2: Dual Ancestry for NaturalPerson

Decision: erp-natural-person specializes both erp-party and erp-person (two generalization-relator entries in its taxonomy).

Rationale: NaturalPerson must inherit legal capacity from the Party chain (agreements, accounts) and social identity from the Person chain (demographics, contact info). Without dual ancestry, these properties would need to be duplicated or bridged with relators.

Affected apps: Party Master (Layer 0).

D3: No erp-organization Universal

Decision: The legal entity concept lives in Party Master as erp-legal-entity, not as a separate universal in Org Structure.

Rationale: Having both erp-organization and erp-legal-entity would create an identity split: two first-class entities for one real-world company that must stay synchronized. Instead, the top-level erp-business-unit serves as the org hierarchy root and is optionally linked to its erp-legal-entity via a cross-block attribution relator.

Affected apps: Org Structure, Party Master.

D4: Aggregation for Org Hierarchy (not Composition)

Decision: Org unit parent-child relationships use aggregation-relator, not composition-relator.

Rationale: Departments and teams survive organizational restructuring. A department moved from one business unit to another retains its identity, history, and cost center assignments. Composition would imply that children are destroyed when the parent is dissolved.

Affected apps: Org Structure. Same pattern used for GL Account hierarchy in Chart of Accounts.

D5: Composition for Fiscal Calendar

Decision: Fiscal Year → Quarter → Period uses composition-relator.

Rationale: A fiscal quarter cannot exist independently of its fiscal year; a fiscal period cannot exist independently of its quarter. Deleting a fiscal year should cascade-delete its quarters and periods. This is a textbook composition relationship and enables auto-instantiation via Model1's composition framework.

Affected apps: Fiscal Calendar.

D6: Business Unit vs Legal Entity

Decision: Business units are organizational units in Org Structure (not Party Master). They represent P&L responsibility, not legal personhood.

Rationale: A corporation (legal entity) may contain multiple business units. A business unit may span multiple legal entities in a group structure. These are fundamentally different concepts: one is about legal capacity, the other about management accountability. Conflating them limits modeling flexibility for multi-entity enterprises.

Affected apps: Org Structure, Party Master.

D7: Resource Catalog Generalizes Beyond Items

Decision: The traditional "item master" is replaced with a 3-level resource hierarchy covering tangible goods, intangible services/IP, and capacity resources.

Rationale: Modern ERP must track services (consulting, SaaS), information resources (documents, IP), financial instruments (receivables, securities), and capacity (person-hours, machine-hours) alongside physical inventory. The shared erp-resource root enables "total resource consumption by department" artifacts that span all resource types.

Affected apps: Resource Catalog, all consumers (Procurement, Project Mgmt, etc.).

D8: Location Includes Address Infrastructure

Decision: The Location Master app includes structured address universals (erp-address, erp-address-type) in addition to the geographic hierarchy (Country → Region → Site).

Rationale: Addresses are inherently geographic artifacts. Including them in Location rather than Party avoids address duplication across blocks and enables cross-block references (Party → Address, Site → Address) from a single source.

Affected apps: Location, Party (consumer via cross-block reference).

D9: Goal as Foundation Universal

Decision: erp-goal is defined as an abstract base universal that can be shared across domain apps (OKR objectives/key results, budget targets, KPIs, SLAs).

Rationale: Goal attainment analytics must span strategic (OKR), operational (KPI/SLA), and financial (budget target) domains. A shared root enables cross-domain aggregation artifacts like "goal attainment by department across all goal types."

Affected apps: OKR (currently defines it locally; should migrate to a shared foundation).

D10: Process as Universal for Budget and OKR Workflows

Decision: Budget approval (3-stage) and OKR planning/review (3-stage each) are defined as process universals with composed stage universals, not hardcoded workflow logic.

Rationale: Using the Process-as-Universal pattern allows the ProcessEngine to derive capabilities from composition depth (tier model). Budget approval is Tier 2 (stages + obligations), OKR Planning is Tier 1 (stages only), and OKR Review is Tier 2 (stages + obligations). All three use FunctionalRole universals for authority assignment.

Affected apps: Budget (1 process, 3 FunctionalRoles), OKR (2 processes, 3 FunctionalRoles).

What Domain Apps Do NOT Contain

Domain apps (app_erp_org through app_erp_okr) do not define organizations, tenants, or users. These are inherited from app_erp_base, which must be loaded first. Each domain app contains only:
  • Its own app definition (app, module, service)
  • Its own universals, properties, relators
  • Its own computations, events, artifacts (if applicable)
  • App-scoped foundation_role_assignments that grant the shared users access to this specific app
The foundation_role_assignments section references user IDs defined in app_erp_base. The loader resolves these cross-file references because it pre-populates users from MemDB before processing.

11. Common Metadata Pitfalls & Lessons Learned

During the metadata fixing process for the ERP suite, numerous validation and loading errors were encountered and resolved. This section documents the specific errors and their fixes to serve as a reference for future metadata app development.

11.1 Relator Instance Field Names

CategoryWrongCorrectContext
Relator instances (data files) "relator_universal_id": "..." "relator_id": "..." In metadata_app_data.json, relator instances use relator_id to reference the relator universal, not relator_universal_id.
Computation inputs (role_aggregate source) "relator_universal_id": "..." "role_id": "..." When a computation input uses source: "role_aggregate", the role reference field is role_id.
Computation inputs (aggregate function) "aggregation": "Sum" "function": "Sum" The aggregate function field in computation inputs is named function, not aggregation.

11.2 Null Values — Omit, Do Not Set to Null

WrongCorrectExplanation
"reference_universal_id": null Omit the field entirely Setting optional fields to null causes deserialization errors. If a field is not applicable, remove it from the JSON object.
"state_property_id": null (in thresholds) Omit or provide a real property ID Artifact threshold definitions require either a valid state_property_id or the field should be omitted. The validator rejects null.

11.3 Invalid Enum Values

FieldInvalid ValueValid ValuesFix Applied
Artifact measure function CountWhere Sum, Count, Avg, Min, Max Changed to Count. Conditional counting is not a supported aggregate function.
Artifact dimension source_type DirectProperty Property, MeasurementExtract, RelatorPath, Constant Changed to Property.
Computation trigger trigger_type RelatorEstablished, RelatorDissolved RelatorLifecycle with lifecycle sub-field Changed to "trigger_type": "RelatorLifecycle" with "lifecycle": "Created" or "lifecycle": "Deleted". Valid lifecycle phases: Created, Modified, Deleted.

11.4 Cross-App Seed ID Collisions

Problem: When multiple domain apps use the same generic seed ID (e.g., erp-summary-threshold-state-prop), loading the second app fails with "Record already exists" because UUID v5 generates identical UUIDs from identical seeds within the same namespace.

Fix: Namespace all seed IDs per app. For example, use erp-resource-threshold-state-prop instead of a generic erp-summary-threshold-state-prop. Each property definition ID should include the domain prefix to guarantee uniqueness across the suite.

11.5 Cross-File User References

Problem: Domain apps need to grant the shared ERP users access via foundation_role_assignments, but those users are defined in app_erp_base. Without the users being present in MemDB when the domain app loads, the user_id references in role assignments cannot resolve.

Fix: The loader was enhanced to pre-populate users and apps from MemDB before processing each metadata file. Domain apps now use foundation_role_assignments (a top-level array in metadata_app.json) that references user IDs by their seed-derived UUIDs. The base app must be loaded first so that users exist in MemDB when domain apps are processed.
