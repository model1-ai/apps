ERP Organizational Structure Layer 0

Org Structure is the skeleton of the enterprise. It is a Layer 0 block that is independently deployable -- it can be loaded as a separate metadata app without requiring other blocks to exist first. Every other ERP domain (HR, Finance, Procurement, Projects) references org structure.

1. Overview

The Org Structure block answers three fundamental questions about the enterprise:

QuestionModeled ByExamples
Where does work happen? Departments, Teams, Committees Engineering, Platform Team, Steering Committee
Who is accountable? Business Units (P&L responsibility) Technology Services, Operations
Where does money go? Cost Centers (allocation targets) CC-1001 Engineering Operations, CC-9001 Shared IT
Layer 0 Independence: Because it is dependency-free, Org Structure can be deployed, validated, and populated before any other ERP block exists. It has one optional cross-block reference to erp-legal-entity in Party Master via an association relator, established only when both blocks are deployed.

The block defines 10 universals (4 org-unit specializations, 3 supporting entities, 2 enumerations, 1 artifact output), 28 property definitions, 4 domain-specific relators, 2 event definitions, and 1 artifact definition (Headcount by Department).

3. Generalization Hierarchy

All org-unit types share a common abstract base (erp-org-unit) via generalization relators. The base defines shared properties (code, name, dates, status) that every specialization inherits. Each specialization adds only properties unique to its nature.

Standalone entities: erp-cost-center, erp-position, and erp-job-grade do not generalize erp-org-unit. They are connected to the org hierarchy via relators (attribution and aggregation), not via inheritance.

9. Design Decision Points

9.1 No erp-organization Universal

Org Structure deliberately omits a separate "organization" or "company" universal. The legal entity (corporation, partnership, etc.) is modeled in Party Master as erp-legal-entity. Having both erp-organization and erp-legal-entity as first-class entities for one real-world company creates an identity split -- two entities that must stay synchronized. Instead, Party Master owns the legal identity ("who"), and Org Structure owns the internal management hierarchy ("how it's structured"). The two are linked via an optional many-to-many association relator.

9.2 Business Unit Is NOT a Legal Entity

Business units are strategic/management constructs with P&L responsibility, not synonymous with legal entities. The relationship is many-to-many:

  • One legal entity, multiple BUs: A corporation may contain separate business units for different product lines.
  • One BU, multiple legal entities: A multinational business unit may span subsidiaries in different jurisdictions.
  • One-to-one (simple case): A small company with one BU and one legal entity -- a special case, not the general rule.

The cross-layer association relator is typed to erp-org-unit (not erp-business-unit specifically) because departments and teams can also be associated with legal entities in complex structures.

9.3 Aggregation for Hierarchy (not Composition)

The recursive erp-unit-parent-rel uses aggregation because org restructuring moves units between parents without destroying them. A department can be re-parented from one business unit to another without being deleted and recreated. Composition would imply that deleting the parent destroys all children -- exactly the wrong semantics for restructuring.

9.4 Aggregation for Position-Department (not Composition)

erp-dept-has-position-rel uses aggregation because positions have independent significance:

  • When a department is dissolved, positions move to a successor department or are explicitly frozen/closed -- they do not silently vanish.
  • Historical staffing analysis requires knowing which positions existed in a department at a given time.
  • A position marked FROZEN retains organizational significance regardless of what happens to its parent department.

9.5 Cost Center Separate from Department

Cost centers are independent entities, not properties of departments:

  • A project-based cost center may span multiple departments.
  • A shared services cost center (IT infrastructure, facilities) may not correspond to any single department.
  • One department can map to multiple cost centers (e.g., R&D with separate cost centers per product line).

The sample data demonstrates this: CC-9001 Shared IT Infrastructure exists without any department mapping.

9.6 Manager Is a Role, Not a Universal

Manager is modeled at Layer 1 via a role-assignment-relator connecting a Person (from Party Master / Employee block) to an OrgUnit with a FunctionalRole universal. This keeps Layer 0 free of person-dependent concepts. Position represents a slot ("Engineering Director"), not the person filling it.

9.7 Team vs Department Distinction

Both specialize erp-org-unit and participate in the same analytical aggregations. The distinction:

  • Department: Permanent functional grouping with a defined function (Engineering, Finance, HR).
  • Team: Typically cross-functional and may be temporary (erp-team-is-permanent-prop). Can be dissolved without restructuring the department hierarchy.

Both appear as children in erp-unit-parent-rel, enabling mixed hierarchies (e.g., Platform Team reports into Engineering department).
