ERP Party Master Layer 0

Block: Party Master — the universal "who" of the enterprise.
Layer: 0 (independently deployable, no hard structural dependencies).
App ID: erp-party-master-app
Optional cross-block reference: Location Master's erp-address via erp-party-has-address-rel (aggregation).

Party Master provides the shared identity foundation that every other ERP domain references when it needs to answer "who is involved?" Every actor that can enter agreements, hold accounts, or participate in transactions is a Party. A party may be a Natural Person (a human being with legal capacity) or a Legal Entity (a corporation, partnership, government body, or non-profit organization).

The block has two distinctive features:

  1. Deep generalization chains with a dual ancestry pattern for Natural Person, providing analytical power across biological, demographic, social, and legal domains.
  2. Strict party-role separation — Employee, Vendor, and Customer are roles assigned via role-assignment-relator at Layer 1, not generalization specializations of Party.

2. Dual Generalization Hierarchy

Party Master contains two parallel generalization chains that converge at erp-natural-person. Chain A (the Person chain) models biological-to-social identity. Chain B (the Party chain) models legal capacity and organizational form. Natural Person inherits from both chains simultaneously — this is the dual ancestry pattern.

Key insight: erp-natural-person has two generalization-relator entries in its taxonomy — one pointing to erp-party and one pointing to erp-person. This means every NaturalPerson instance carries properties from both the Person chain (biological/demographic/social) and the Party chain (legal/commercial identity).

Property Conflict Resolution

The two chains contribute disjoint property sets — there are no property ID collisions. Property IDs are globally unique (e.g., erp-party-code-prop vs erp-human-full-name-prop), so every property resolves to exactly one definition without ambiguity.

ChainPropertiesDomain
A (Person chain)kingdom, species, birth_date, death_date, full_name, gender, nationality, email, phone, government_id, preferred_languageBiological and social identity
B (Party chain)party_code, display_name, tax_id, is_active, notesLegal/commercial identity
Convention: For overlapping semantic concerns (not ID collisions), Chain A wins for biological/demographic attributes and Chain B wins for legal/transactional attributes. In practice no conflicts exist because the property sets are disjoint by design.

8. Design Decisions

Decision 1: Dual Ancestry for NaturalPerson

erp-natural-person has two generalization-relator entries: one to erp-party and one to erp-person. This is a fixed, structural join point between two orthogonal classification axes (biological identity vs legal capacity) — not a dynamic assignment.

Why this is not multiple inheritance: A natural person is always both a person and a party. You cannot be one without the other. Compare with party roles (Employee, Vendor, Customer) which are dynamic and overlapping — a person can be an Employee without being a Vendor. That distinction makes roles a multiple-inheritance hazard; dual ancestry for NaturalPerson is a structural identity statement.

Conflict resolution: The two chains contribute disjoint property sets. Chain A owns biological/demographic properties; Chain B owns legal/transactional properties. No property ID collisions exist by design.

Decision 2: Party-Role Separation

Employee, Vendor, and Customer are not generalization specializations of Party. They are roles assigned via role-assignment-relator at Layer 1.

Rationale: A single party can simultaneously be an Employee, a Vendor, and a Customer. Modeling these as generalizations would require multiple inheritance with conflicting properties (billing terms from Vendor vs payroll frequency from Employee). The role pattern avoids the diamond problem entirely: one party, multiple role instances, each carrying role-specific properties without polluting the party entity.

Decision 3: No erp-party-type Enum

Legal form (individual, corporation, partnership, government body, non-profit) is expressed through the specialization hierarchy itself. An instance's universal is its type: creating an erp-corporation instance means the party is a corporation.

A redundant erp-party-type enum would create two competing sources of truth for the same fact (the hierarchy says "corporation" but the type attribute could say "partnership").

Decision 4: Aggregation for Party → Address

Addresses are linked via aggregation (not composition) because they require authored content (street, city, postal code). Auto-instantiating empty address shells when a party is created adds no ontological value. Addresses are created with their content and then linked to the party. Application-layer cleanup handles orphaned addresses.

erp-address itself is defined in Location Master. Party Master only defines the linking relator erp-party-has-address-rel.

Decision 5: Aggregation for Party → Contact

Contact channels are linked via aggregation for the same reason as addresses: they require authored content (a phone number, an email address). Auto-instantiating empty contact records serves no purpose. While contacts are semantically dependent on their owner, the relator type reflects the authored nature of the content.

Decision 6: NaturalPerson is Terminal Specialization

erp-natural-person has no own properties and no further specializations at Layer 0. It exists solely as the structural bridge between the Person chain and the Party hierarchy. Any further specialization of natural persons (e.g., minor, estate) would be modeled at higher layers if needed.

Decision 7: LegalEntity Specializations Cover Major Forms

The four specializations — erp-corporation, erp-partnership, erp-government-body, erp-non-profit — cover the major organizational forms encountered in enterprise systems. Each can carry type-specific properties (e.g., stock ticker for corporations) while inheriting the shared legal entity properties (legal name, registration number, jurisdiction).

At Layer 0, erp-partnership, erp-government-body, and erp-non-profit have no additional properties. Type-specific attributes are deferred to Layer 1 where they connect to domain-specific blocks (compliance, budgeting, grant management).
