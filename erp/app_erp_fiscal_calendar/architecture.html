ERP Fiscal Calendar Layer 0

1. Overview

The Fiscal Calendar defines the time structure for financial reporting, budget periods, and compliance deadlines. It is a Layer 0 block -- independently deployable, with zero external dependencies. Every time-bounded financial concept (budgets, journal entries, reporting periods, agreement validity) references the fiscal calendar, but the fiscal calendar references nothing outside itself.

The block answers three fundamental questions:

  • When does the accounting year start and end? Fiscal years may differ from calendar years (April-March, July-June, October-September).
  • What are the reporting periods? Quarters and periods (typically months) within the fiscal year that define when financial statements are produced.
  • Which periods accept postings? Period status controls whether transactions can be recorded, preventing inadvertent posting to closed periods.
Composition-heavy design: The calendar hierarchy uses composition relators throughout. Creating a single fiscal year via create_instance_with_composition auto-instantiates all 4 quarters and their 12-13 periods, producing the full calendar structure in a single operation.

2. Composition Hierarchy

The fiscal calendar is built on a strict two-level composition tree. Each level uses a composition relator (taxonomy target: composition-relator), meaning child entities cannot exist independently of their parent. Deletion cascades downward.

Auto-Instantiation Behavior

When a Fiscal Year instance is created via create_instance_with_composition, the MemDB composition auto-instantiation system recursively creates:

  1. 4 Fiscal Quarter instances (Q1 through Q4), each linked to the year by an erp-year-has-quarter-rel composition relator instance.
  2. For each quarter, 3 Fiscal Period instances (regular months), each linked to its quarter by an erp-quarter-has-period-rel composition relator instance.
  3. Q4 receives an additional P13 adjustment period (4 periods total in Q4).
Total entities per fiscal year: 1 Year + 4 Quarters + 13 Periods + 4 Year-Quarter relator instances + 13 Quarter-Period relator instances = 35 entities created from a single operation.
Sample data uses skip_composition: true on fiscal year and quarter instances in metadata_app_data.json because all composed instances are explicitly defined in the file. This prevents the loader from auto-instantiating duplicates.

3. Temporal Nature

Fiscal years, quarters, and periods are modeled as Perdurant (event-like, time-bounded) because they represent stretches of time with definite start and end dates. They come into existence, persist for a defined duration, and then end. This distinguishes them from Endurant entities (like GL accounts or the fiscal status enum) that exist continuously without temporal boundaries.

Universal Temporal Nature Rationale
erp-fiscal-year Perdurant A 12-month window with definite start/end. FY2025 began on Jan 1, 2025 and ended Dec 31, 2025.
erp-fiscal-quarter Perdurant A 3-month window within a fiscal year. Q1 of FY2026 spans Jan-Mar 2026.
erp-fiscal-period Perdurant Typically a calendar month. The atomic time unit for financial transactions.
erp-fiscal-status Endurant Enumeration of status values (Open, Closed, Adjustment, Locked). These labels exist continuously and are referenced, not time-bounded.

6. State Machine — Fiscal Status Transitions

Period and year status controls whether financial transactions can be posted. The transition rules enforce a strictly forward lifecycle. At Layer 0 we define the states and semantics; enforcement logic (rejecting postings to closed periods) belongs to Layer 1 when journal entry processing is implemented.

9. Decision Points

Composition for Year - Quarter - Period

Decision: Use composition relators (not aggregation) for both Year-Quarter and Quarter-Period relationships.

Rationale: The lifecycle of child entities is fully dependent on the parent. "Q3" only makes sense as "Q3 of FY2026." If FY2026 is deleted (e.g., created in error before any transactions), its quarters must be destroyed. Similarly, "P07 - July" exists only within "Q3 of FY2026." This makes auto-instantiation appropriate: creating a fiscal year deterministically produces its full structural tree of quarters and periods.

Alternative considered: Aggregation would allow quarters and periods to exist independently, which is semantically incorrect for fiscal calendar structures.

Perdurant Temporal Nature

Decision: All calendar entities (year, quarter, period) are Perdurant.

Rationale: These entities represent stretches of time with definite start and end dates. FY2025 began on January 1, 2025 and ended on December 31, 2025. It came into existence, persisted for 12 months, and then ended. This is the hallmark of a Perdurant entity, as opposed to Endurant entities like GL accounts that exist continuously without temporal boundaries.

Period 13 Support

Decision: Include a 13th adjustment period (P13) under Q4.

Rationale: Many accounting standards and enterprise systems (SAP, Oracle Financials) require a 13th period for year-end adjustments (accruals, deferrals, reclassifications). P13 is structurally a Q4 period with is_adjustment_period = true and sequence 13. Its date range intentionally overlaps P12 (same end date as the fiscal year). This gives clean period-over-period comparisons because adjustment entries are isolated from regular period activity.

Structural note: There is no direct Year-Period composition. P13 lives under Q4 via the erp-quarter-has-period-rel relator, making Q4 the only quarter with 4 periods instead of 3.

Fiscal Year May Differ from Calendar Year

Decision: Decouple fiscal year from calendar year via explicit start_date and end_date properties.

Rationale: Organizations worldwide use different fiscal year boundaries:

  • US federal government: October 1 - September 30
  • UK tax year: April 6 - April 5
  • Japanese fiscal year: April 1 - March 31
  • Retail 4-5-4 calendar: Variable week-based periods

The code property (FY2026) follows the convention of naming by the year in which the fiscal year ends, which is the most common international convention.

11. Event Definitions

The fiscal calendar module publishes two event types when fiscal statuses change. These events enable higher-layer blocks (journal entry processing, reporting) to react to period opens/closes without polling.

Event ID Label Scope Payload Fields
erp-period-status-changed-event Period Status Changed tenant period_id (uuid), period_code (string), fiscal_year_code (string), old_status (string), new_status (string)
erp-year-status-changed-event Year Status Changed tenant year_id (uuid), year_code (string), old_status (string), new_status (string)
The app module (erp-fiscal-calendar-module) declares these as publishable_events but subscribes to no events -- consistent with Layer 0 having no upstream dependencies.
